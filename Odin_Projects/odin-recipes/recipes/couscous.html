couscous

Description about Couscous

Moroccan couscous is a culinary masterpiece that stands as a symbol of tradition and culinary artistry in Moroccan cuisine. This dish revolves around fluffy, steamed couscous grains, meticulously prepared to achieve a light and airy texture. Often served as a base for a variety of stews, the couscous acts as a perfect canvas for absorbing the rich flavors of its accompanying ingredients.

Dish ingrediants:

  • Lamb, chicken, or beef (for the stew)
  • Carrots
  • Zucchini
  • Chickpeas
  • Cumin

preparing steps

  1. Prepare the couscous according to package instructions.
  2. Season the meat (lamb, chicken, or beef) with cumin, coriander, turmeric, and cinnamon.
  3. In a large pot, brown the seasoned meat.
  4. Add chopped carrots, zucchini, and chickpeas to the pot.
  5. Cook the vegetables until they start to soften.
  6. Add water or broth to the pot, creating a flavorful stew. Simmer until the meat is tender.
  7. Fluff the cooked couscous with a fork.
  8. Serve the couscous in bowls and ladle the stew over the top.
  9. Garnish with fresh herbs, raisins, and toasted almonds.
  10. Enjoy your delicious Moroccan couscous!
