Moroccan cuisine

Moroccan cuisine is a rich tapestry of flavors, colors, and aromatic spices that reflect the country's diverse cultural influences. Characterized by a harmonious blend of Berber,
Arab, and Mediterranean culinary traditions, Moroccan food is renowned for its exotic and vibrant dishes. Tagine, a slow-cooked stew named after the earthenware pot it's cooked in, is a culinary icon, featuring tender meats or vegetables infused with a symphony of spices like cumin, coriander, and saffron.
