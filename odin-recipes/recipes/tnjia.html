Description about Tanjia

Moroccan Tanjia is a culinary treasure that hails from the vibrant city of Marrakech. This dish embodies the heart and soul of Moroccan cuisine, showcasing the art of slow-cooking with aromatic spices. Tanjia is traditionally prepared by slow-cooking marinated meat in a special clay pot, resulting in tender and flavorful goodness.

Dish Ingredients:

  • Lamb shoulder or beef shank
  • Preserved lemons
  • Garlic
  • Cumin
  • Coriander
  • Paprika
  • Cayenne pepper
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper

Preparing Steps:

  1. Marinate the meat with crushed garlic, cumin, coriander, paprika, cayenne pepper, and salt. Add a generous amount of olive oil.
  2. Make incisions in the meat and stuff them with slices of preserved lemons.
  3. Place the marinated meat in a Tanjia pot, ensuring it's well-coated with the marinade.
  4. Seal the pot with a piece of parchment paper and then cover it tightly with foil.
  5. Cook the Tanjia in a low-temperature oven or in the ashes of a communal wood-fired oven for several hours, allowing the flavors to meld and the meat to become incredibly tender.
  6. Once cooked, serve the Tanjia with Moroccan bread and enjoy the unique and aromatic flavors.
